Qutub Minar is a minaret located in the Qutub complex in Delhi, India. Qutub Minar is the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at a height of 73 meters (239.5 feet).The construction of Qutub Minar was started by Qutub-ud-din Aibak, the first Muslim ruler of Delhi, in 1192. However, the minaret was completed by his successor, Iltutmish, in 1220.


Qutub Minar is made of red sandstone and marble and is covered with intricate carvings and inscriptions. The carvings include verses from the Quran, as well as Hindu and Jain motifs.
