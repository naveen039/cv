Golkonda, also spelled Golconda, is a historic citadel and fortress located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. The name Golkonda is believed to have been derived from two Telugu words, "Golla" and "Konda," which mean "shepherd's hill." This is because the area around the fort was once inhabited by shepherds.


The Golkonda Fort was originally built as a mud fort during the reign of the Kakatiya dynasty in the 12th century. It was later fortified by the Qutb Shahi dynasty in the 16th century.Golkonda was a prominent center of diamond trade in India during the medieval period. The Koh-i-Noor diamond, which is now part of the British Crown Jewels, is believed to have been mined from the Golkonda region.


The fort is famous for its acoustics. The main entrance, called the Fateh Darwaza, is a large gate that was built to commemorate the victory of the Qutb Shahi dynasty over the Mughals. The gate is designed in such a way that a handclap at the entrance can be heard at the highest point of the fort, almost a kilometer away.The Golkonda Fort has four distinct forts within its walls. The lowest is the outermost fort, followed by the middle fort, the inner fort, and the highest point, called the Bala Hissar.


The fort is home to several palaces, mosques, and gardens, including the Taramati Baradari, a beautiful pavilion that was once used for music and dance performances. Golkonda Fort is a popular tourist destination in Hyderabad and attracts thousands of visitors every year.
