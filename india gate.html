India Gate is a war memorial located in New Delhi, the capital city of India. It was built to commemorate the sacrifice of Indian soldiers who died in World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War. India Gate was designed by Sir Edwin Lutyens, one of the most renowned architects of his time. He was also the architect behind many other famous buildings in New Delhi, including the Rashtrapati Bhavan, the Parliament House, and the Connaught Place.


The foundation stone of India Gate was laid by the Duke of Connaught, on February 10, 1921, and the monument was officially inaugurated on February 12, 1931.India Gate stands at a height of 42 meters and is made of red sandstone and granite. It is designed in the shape of an arch, with a dome at the top, and is flanked by four turrets.The walls of India Gate are inscribed with the names of over 13,000 soldiers who died in the World War I and the Third Anglo-Afghan War.
