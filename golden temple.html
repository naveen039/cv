The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib, is a prominent Sikh gurdwara located in the city of Amritsar in the state of Punjab, India. The temple is a symbol of Sikhism and its philosophy of equality, tolerance, and social justice.


The temple was first built in 1604 by Guru Arjan Dev, the fifth Sikh Guru, and was later renovated and expanded by other Gurus. The temple is surrounded by a large tank, known as the Amrit Sarovar, which is said to have healing properties. The temple's architecture is a blend of Hindu and Islamic styles, with a central dome covered in gold leaf that gives the temple its name..

The temple also has a museum that displays historical artifacts related to Sikhism and the temple's history.
