The Sun Temple is a temple complex located in Konark, in the Indian state of Odisha. The Sun Temple was built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty.The temple is dedicated to the Hindu sun god, Surya, and is designed in the shape of a chariot with 24 wheels, pulled by seven horses.


The temple is known for its exquisite stone carvings, which depict various scenes from Hindu mythology, as well as everyday life during the time it was built.The temple was originally located on the sea shore, but over time, the sea receded and the temple is now located about 3 km inland.The temple complex consists of the main temple, a Natya Mandapa (dancing hall), and a Bhoga Mandapa (feasting hall).


The main temple is 100 feet high and is built in the Kalinga style of architecture, which is known for its use of stone carvings and intricate designs.The temple is designed in such a way that the first rays of the sun would fall on the main entrance of the temple every day, creating a spectacular sight.The temple was partially destroyed during the 17th century by the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb, who considered it to be a symbol of Hindu pride.


The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered to be one of the Seven Wonders of India.The Sun Temple at Konark is an important pilgrimage site for Hindus and is visited by thousands of devotees every year during the annual Konark Dance Festival, which celebrates classical Indian dance forms.


There are many erotic sculptures and carvings on the walls of the Sun Temple. These carvings depict various sexual positions and acts, and are said to have been added to the temple as a symbol of fertility and abundance.The temple has a unique alignment with the magnetic North Pole, and it is believed that the temple's architects were able to calculate the correct alignment through advanced knowledge of astronomy and mathematics.


The temple is also designed to be a giant sundial, with its shadow marking the time of day. It is said that the temple could be used to tell time accurate to within a minute.There is a hidden chamber beneath the main temple that is said to have contained a large magnet. It is believed that this magnet was used to suspend the main idol of the temple in mid-air.The temple is designed in such a way that it is said to have been built without the use of mortar. The stones are said to be so precisely cut and fitted together that no mortar was required to hold them in place.


The temple was originally built with a massive flagpole on top of it, which was designed to fly a flag that could be seen from miles away. Unfortunately, the flagpole was destroyed during a storm in the 19th century.The temple's chariot shape is said to have been inspired by the mythological chariot of the sun god Surya. The seven horses are said to represent the seven days of the week.The temple's intricate carvings and sculptures are said to have been created by over 1,200 artisans, who worked on the temple for over a decade.The temple is said to have been built using black granite and red sandstone, which were transported to the site from over 30 km away.
