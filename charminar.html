Charminar is a historical monument located in the Old City area of Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It was built by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah in 1591 to commemorate the end of a deadly epidemic that had ravaged the city. The monument is a four-pillared and arch-shaped structure, made of granite and limestone, and stands at a height of approximately 56 meters.


The name Charminar comes from two Urdu words: "Char" meaning four and "minar" meaning tower. The monument has four minarets, each standing at a height of approximately 20 meters, and has a balcony on the second floor that offers a panoramic view of the city.


Charminar is an important landmark and a popular tourist attraction in Hyderabad. It is considered to be a masterpiece of Islamic architecture and is known for its intricate carvings, arches, and domes. The monument also houses a mosque on the top floor, where prayers are offered every day.


The area around Charminar is known for its bustling bazaars and markets, selling everything from traditional textiles and jewelry to spices and street food. The monument and its surroundings are also an important cultural hub and a symbol of the city's rich history and heritage.

There are several inscriptions on the walls of Charminar, but some of them are hidden from view. These inscriptions contain verses from the Quran and other religious texts, as well as details about the construction of the monument.If you stand at a certain point near Charminar and clap your hands, the sound echoes back to you in a unique way. This is because the stones used in the construction of the monument are said to vibrate at a particular frequency.


Charminar is often associated with the mosque on its top floor, but not many people know that there is another mosque nearby, known as Makkah Masjid. This mosque is one of the largest in India and was also built by Sultan Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah.


There is a small museum inside Charminar that displays old photographs of Hyderabad from the time of its construction. These photographs offer a glimpse into the history and evolution of the city over the years.
